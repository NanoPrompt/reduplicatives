Fun Reduplicative Words & Their Meanings

Enjoy this quirky collection of playful English words!

  • Helter-skelter – in a disorderly or chaotic way
  • Wishy-washy – indecisive or weak in opinion
  • Teeny-tiny – very, very small
  • Hocus-pocus – deceptive or magical nonsense
  • Dilly-dally – waste time; procrastinate
  • Pitter-patter – soft, rapid tapping sound (like rain)
  • Shilly-shally – hesitate or waver
  • Chit-chat – casual small talk
  • Riff-raff – disreputable or undesirable people
  • Knick-knack – small decorative trinket
  • Mumbo-jumbo – meaningless or confusing speech
  • Hip-hop – genre of music and dance
  • Zig-zag – sharp turns in alternate directions
  • Tick-tock – sound of a clock ticking
  • Flip-flop – back-and-forth change or a casual sandal
  • Sing-song – rhythmic or singsong tone of voice
  • Boogie-woogie – lively music or dance style
  • Namby-pamby – overly sentimental or weak
  • Heebie-jeebies – feelings of nervousness or fear
  • Tittle-tattle – gossip or idle chatter
  • Hanky-panky – flirtatious or mischievous behavior
  • Willy-nilly – whether one likes it or not; without plan
  • Hunky-dory – going just fine or all right
  • Roly-poly – round and chubby
  • Goody-goody – overly virtuous or smugly obedient
  • Okey-dokey – playful way to say okay
  • Fuddy-duddy – someone old-fashioned or fussy
  • Hurly-burly – chaos or noisy disorder
  • Super-duper – excellent; fantastic
  • Boogie-boogie – energetic dance or music
  • Lovey-dovey – overly affectionate
  • Ticky-tacky – cheap or low-quality
  • Meeny-miney – part of a children's counting rhyme
  • Higgledy-piggledy – jumbled or messy
  • Yada-yada – repetitive or trivial talk
  • See-saw – back-and-forth motion or playground equipment
  • Snip-snap – quick cutting or snapping
  • Clap-trap – nonsense or empty talk
  • Chop-chop – hurry up!
  • Click-clack – rhythmic mechanical sounds
  • Hum-drum – dull or monotonous
  • Mish-mash – disorganized mixture
  • Ping-pong – table tennis
  • Toodle-oo – a cheerful goodbye
  • Tip-top – of the highest quality
  • Hobnob – to socialize with people, especially the elite
  • Criss-cross – intersecting lines or patterns
